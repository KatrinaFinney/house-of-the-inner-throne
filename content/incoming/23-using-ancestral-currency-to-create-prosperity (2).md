## The Inner Law

Prosperity is inherited before it is earned.

Every individual begins life within a network of inherited knowledge, relationships, and opportunities created by those who lived before them. These inheritances may not always appear as money, yet they remain forms of wealth.

Skills, resilience, cultural knowledge, and survival strategies all represent ancestral currency.

Recognizing these inheritances transforms the practitioner's relationship with prosperity.

## A Mirror for the Practitioner

Many people search for prosperity as though it must arrive from outside their lives.

Yet when one examines their own history carefully, signs of inherited strength appear everywhere. Languages spoken, crafts learned, traditions preserved, and struggles survived all form part of a lineage's accumulated wealth.

Acknowledging this inheritance changes how prosperity is pursued.

## The Hidden Mechanism

Gratitude activates circulation.

When a practitioner recognizes the contributions of previous generations, they begin participating in a cycle of reciprocity. Gratitude encourages responsible stewardship, and stewardship allows resources to grow rather than stagnate.

In this way prosperity becomes a relationship rather than a possession.

## Where This Appears in Daily Life

Businesses are often built upon family knowledge.

Communities develop industries passed down through generations. Recipes, agricultural knowledge, trades, and professional skills frequently travel through families and cultures.

These transmissions represent ancestral investments.

## Prosperity as Continuity

The practitioner begins to see prosperity differently.

Wealth becomes something that flows through generations rather than something captured by individuals.

To participate in this flow responsibly is to honor the ancestors.

## The Language of Color

Green represents growth, prosperity, and flourishing opportunity.

Lighting a **green candle** supports intentions related to financial development and sustainable wealth.

## Returning to the Inner Throne

Prosperity grows strongest when rooted in gratitude.

By recognizing the wealth already present within their lineage, the practitioner learns to cultivate prosperity responsibly.

# The Rite of Alignment

## The Intelligence Behind the Lesson

Aje, within Yoruba cosmology, represents the sacred force of prosperity, exchange, and material increase. Aje is not merely money. It is the living current of wealth itself — the movement of value, opportunity, circulation, and increase.

Within the Inner Throne system, Aje represents the intelligence of prosperity as a force that must be cultivated, honored, and directed with discipline.

## Energetic Current

The prosperity current strengthens:

• the cultivation of wealth consciousness  
• receptivity to opportunity  
• disciplined stewardship of material resources  
• the circulation of prosperity through one's life

When this current becomes active, prosperity is no longer approached as luck or accident. It is approached as an energy that can be fed, directed, and expanded.

## Communion Ritual

Place a **green candle** upon your altar.

Beside it place **coins, paper currency, and ancestor money**.

Sit quietly for several breaths and reflect on the reality that prosperity moves through generations. Consider the labor, sacrifices, and knowledge that made your present opportunities possible.

Light the green candle.

Then take the **ancestor money** and burn it carefully in a fire-safe bowl or censer as an offering.

As the paper burns, speak aloud:

“I honor the prosperity that came before me.  
I feed the current of wealth with gratitude and discipline.  
May prosperity grow through my life and bless those connected to me.”

After the ancestor money has burned, place your hands near the flame of the candle and reflect on the kind of prosperity you are now cultivating.

Remain seated for several minutes, allowing the ritual to settle into your awareness.

When the ritual is complete, keep one coin from the altar in your wallet, on your desk, or in your place of business as a sign of ongoing prosperity cultivation.
